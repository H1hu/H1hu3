What is History?

History is the study of change over time, and it covers all aspects of human society.
Political, social, economic, scientific, technological, medical,
cultural, intellectual, religious and military developments are all part of history.

Why is History Important?

Studying history allows us to observe and understand how people and societies behaved.
For example, we are able to evaluate war, even when a nation is at peace, by looking back at previous events.
History provides us with the data that is used to create laws, or theories about various aspects of society.

When Did History Start?

History refers to the time period after the invention of written records in a given culture or society.
Archaeologists have discovered written records in Egypt from as early as 3200 BCE,
which is the accepted date at which history "begins" there.

Three Periods of History:

Ancient History

Ancient History Spans between the first 2900 B.C. and the 7th Century.
It starts with the First ever written record known as the Sumerian Cuneiform.
There are Three Distinct Periods of Ancient History.
Stone age which is when humans first used tools and discovered fire
Bronze age which is the period when humans first used bronze.
And the Iron Age in which humans made the switch between bronze to iron.
In this period of history is the known as the emergence of humans as they
begin to create civilaizations and began to expand across the old world(Afro-Eurasia)
This period holds special events such as the discovery of fire, The discovery of copper,
The creation of steel and the expansion across Europe and Asia

Medival History

The Medival History or The Middle Ages spans from the approxiamtely the 5th Century to the 15th Century. The Period started with the fall of the Western Roman Empire and Ended with The Fall of Constantinople during May 1453. This Period is generally split into three ages. Early, High, and Late Middle Ages. The Early Middle Ages of the Dark Age was the Age in which there were no Roman emperors in the west and war was very common. The High Middle Ages was the Age in which Europes Population Exploded which brought social and political changes resulting in the Preceeding Era's Renaissance. The Late Middle Ages is the Final Period of Medival History which was Also known as the Renaissance. This period boasted an explosion of new cultures and ideas which sets up the following Period of History. This Period boasted a numerous amount of Historicaly important events and discoveries. From the Renaissance Setting the world up for the Modern Era. To the Rise and fall of Empires shaping the continent Along with the Voyage across the atlantic leading to the discovery of the New World.

Modern History

The Modern History is the period after Medival History spanning between the 1500 A.D. to the present. Starting with the Industrial Revolution the world saw a drastic change in all aspects of life. Following the Industrial Revolution was the Napoleonic Era which saw Napoleon Dominate the european landscape for over 3 decades spreading revolutionary ideals and changing the balance of power in europe forever. Following which was the Second Industrial Revolution leading into the first world war. This event marked a turning point for humanity as they steered towards the great depression and the second world war. After the devastating WW2 the third Industrial Revolution began alongside which was the Cold War in which the world was split into to two factions And borders were drew by politics not by cultures. After the end of the Cold War the Fourth Industrial Revolution brought upon the Present in which we are in. This period is known for its instability and wars which shaped the planet forever and The Industrial Revolution bringing new technologies and new threats like global warming and nuclear bombs
